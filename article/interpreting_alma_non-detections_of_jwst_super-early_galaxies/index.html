Interpreting ALMA non-detections of JWST super-early galaxies

galaxies, 2022(year), 12(month)

First author: M. Kohandel

Recent attempts to detect OIII 88$\mu$m emission from super-early ($z>10$) galaxy candidates observed by JWST have been unsuccessful. By using zoom-in simulations, we show that these galaxies are faint, and mostly fall below the local metal-poor $[OIII]-SFR$ relation as a result of their low ionization parameter, $U_{\rm ion}\lesssim 10^{-3}$. Such low $U_{\rm ion}$ values are found in galaxies that are in an early assembly stage, and whose stars are still embedded in high-density natal clouds. However, the most luminous galaxy in our sample ($\rm{log}[L_{\rm{[OIII]}}/L_\odot] = 8.4$, $U_{\rm ion} \approx 0.1$) could be detected by ALMA in only $2.8$ hrs.
